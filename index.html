About Me

I'm currently a PhD candidate in Geotechnical Engineering at the University of Melbourne. I specialize in fluid flow in granular materials, CFD-DEM simulations, Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM) modelling, pore network modeling, and machine learning applications in geomechanics. I previously obtained my master's and bachelor's degrees in civil engineering, with a focus on tunnelling, from Beijing Jiaotong University in China.

Research Works

  • Hydraulic Behaviours in Granular Assemblies – LBM modelling, 3D-printed experiments, CT scanning & image processing, and pore-network modelling.
  • Internal Erosion in Gap-Graded Soils – CFD-DEM simulations and deep learning-based anomaly detection.
  • Tunnelling Engineering – Tunnel face stability under seepage, combining DEM simulation and physical modelling.
  • TiGeoNet – A Taichi-accelerated framework for pore-scale network feature extraction.
